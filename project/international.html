International Travel

International Travel is Nearly as Easy as Domestic

Open borders allow you to treat Western Europe like one big country

Due to the European Union and the Schengen Area, it is extremely easy to travel between countries in Europe. With the exception of the United Kingdom, there are no customs or border checks. You just get on the train and go!

The main ways that international travel differs from domestic are in the choices you have and in reliability. International routes sometimes are ran by multiple operators, usually the national operator of both the originating and terminating country. For example, trains between The Netherlands and Germany may be operated by Nederlandse Spoorwegen, Deutsche Bahn, or a partnership of the two. While this adds some complexity to picking a train, it also gives you the choice between operators, which is less common for domestic travel. Using the previous example, you may prefer the punctuality and reliability of Nederlandse Spoorwegen or the comfort of Deutsche Bahn. International travel is often less reliable than domestic travel. This is often caused by delays in one country having a ripple effect on its trains getting to another country.

Apart from this, international travel is pretty much the same as domestic travel. See our domestic travel page and our EURail page below.
