Eurostar

Western Europe's High Speed Connector

Trains that beat the plane

For traveling between Western European hubs, it is hard to be Eurostar. Eurostar is high-speed train operator originally owned by the national railroads of France, Belgium, and the United Kingdom. It's main routes connect London, Paris, Brussels, Amsterdam, and Rotterdam; with additional connections throughout France and the Ruhr in Germany. Depending on the route, Eurostar trains can reach speeds nearing 190 mile-per-hour!

Service is quite frequent, especially on popular routes. Eurostar operates trains every hour from London to Paris for most of the day. Amsterdam to Paris is offered 10 times a day! Eurostar trains are often faster than taking an airplane between cities. While the time in motion is shorter for the plane, you don't have to go to the airport, check your bags, get through TSA, taxi to and from the runway, get your bags, then get back to the city. Especially since airports in Europe tend to be pretty far from the city they serve, the Eurostar almost always makes more sense than the plane.

Eurostar trains can get a bit pricy, with London to Paris tickets starting around $150 for trips within the next few weeks. There are some tricks to getting tickets cheaper though. First, the EURail pass can reduce the price. For a Eurostar train from London to Paris that at time of writing starts at $229, you can get for around $40 with an EURail pass (though you have to buy the pass). Planning in advance can also bring the price down. If you have the luxury of scheduling a couple months in advance, you can snag a ticket from London to Paris from around $70 to $85.
