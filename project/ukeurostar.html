UK Eurostar Travel

How to take the train from the United Kingdom to Continental Europe

With the UK leaving the European Union and the Schengen Area, travel between the UK and Europe has become harder. Travelers now have to go through border checks and customs in order to go to and from the UK.

Bring your passport, ID, and visa or ETA if necessary. Border and luggage checks occur at the originating station, so you will have to arrive to the train early. While the gates close for standard passengers 30 minutes before departure and 15 for business passengers, Eurostar suggests earlier times.
These earlier times are:

  • London: 90 minutes
  • Paris: 90-120 minutes
  • Brussels: 90-120 minutes
  • Amsterdam: 60-90 minutes
  • Rotterdam: 60-90 minutes
  • Lille: 60 minutes

Know whether or not you need a visa. This is based on the destination country. For the EU, citizens of many countries, include the US, do not need a visa. The UK has a website that will tell you what visa, if any, you need. This website goes over what needs to be declared at customs when going between the UK and EU.

There are three classes on trains to an from London; standard, standard premier, and business premier.
Standard Ticket Features:

  • 2 bags plus 1 piece of hand luggage
  • Free Wifi
  • Access to cafe (food and drink not included)
  • Free ticket changes up to 7 days before journey (Excluding fare differences)
Standard Premier Ticket Features:
  • All Standard Ticket Features
  • Included meal served at your seat
  • Larger seat
  • Free magazines
Business Premier Ticket Features:
  • 3 bags plus 1 piece of hand luggage
  • Provided three course meals and champagne
  • Largest seats
  • Free newspapers and magazines
  • Dedicated ticket gates
  • Exclusive Lounges at select stations
  • Ticket changes up until departure
