Domestic Travel

Travel Within A Single Nation

Varies by nation, but usually very good

Domestic train travel is often easy, but how it works varies by nation. Most nations have a national operator that runs most trains, while most also have some private rail companies. The United Kingdom is the only country in Western Europe where the majority of domestic train routes are not operated by a state operator. For the rest of Western Europe, this is a list to the main operator in each nation:

  • Austria: Österreichische Bundesbahnen
  • Belgium: Nationale Maatschappij der Belgische Spoorwegen
  • Denmark: Danske Statsbaner
  • France: Société nationale des chemins de fer français
  • Germany: Deutsche Bahn
  • Italy: Trenitalia
  • The Netherlands: Nederlandse Spoorwegen
  • Spain: Renfe
  • Switzerland: Schweizische Bundesbahnen

Prices, reliability, and quality will differ by nation. French and Swiss trains tend to be high quality and reliable, but are expensive. German trains are usually well priced and comfortable, but unreliable, contrary to stereotypes about German efficiency. Dutch trains are cheap and reliable, but the ride leaves a little to be desired.

These concerns are only concerns compared to other European railways though. While Deutsche Bahn is unreliable compared to NS or SNCF, they have a reliability that Amtrak in the US or VIA Rail in Canada could only dream of. It is likewise for pricing issues.

The train is usually the best option for travel within a nation in Europe. They cost less than flights, are usually shorter than flights, are better for the environment than flight or cars, you don't have to constantly pay attention like with a car, and you get to look out at beautiful landscapes and historic cities.
